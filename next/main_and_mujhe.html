میں is subject case only, مجھے is for object cases, and it comes in three versions for different levels of language (style): مجھے (literary and educated), مجھ کو (everyday usage), and میرے کو (substandard, but also widely used)

مجھے and میں both refer to the self, but they are used in different contexts. مجھے is used to express that something is happening to or for the speaker, while میں is used to refer to the speaker as the subject of a sentence. For example, مجھے khana pasand hai means I like food, while میں khana kha raha hoon means I am eating food.
